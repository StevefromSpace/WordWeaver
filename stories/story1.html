The Oak Tree and the Bamboo

(Level: Easy)

Once upon a time, in a wide, green meadow, grew a great, strong Oak Tree. His trunk was thick, his branches were wide, and his leaves were dark green. The Oak was very proud. "Look at me!" he would boom in a deep, woody voice. "I am the strongest tree in the world! I am mighty!"

Next to him grew a slender, green Bamboo. Bamboo was tall and thin. When the wind blew, he would dance and sway and rustle his leaves.

The proud Oak Tree often laughed at the Bamboo. "You are so thin and weak," the Oak would say. "You bow and bend to every little breeze. You should be strong, like me! Stand up straight! Never bend!"

The Bamboo just smiled his rustling, leafy smile. "Oh, Mr. Oak," he whispered. "Your strength is wonderful, but I like to sway. Bending is how I dance with the wind."

"Nonsense!" huffed the Oak. "Bending is for the weak."

One day, a great storm came. The sky turned dark gray. The wind didn't just whistle; it ROARED. The rain didn't just fall; it POURED.

"Ha!" boomed the Oak Tree, trying to sound brave. "I am not afraid! I am strong! I will not bend!" He spread his branches wide and held his trunk firm against the raging wind. The wind pushed and pushed. The Oak strained and groaned, holding his ground with all his might. He would not move an inch.

Beside him, the Bamboo was dancing wildly. The wind howled, and the Bamboo bent. He bent so low that his leafy top almost touched the wet ground. He swayed and whipped back and forth, but he did not fight the wind. He simply bent with it.

"Poor, weak Bamboo!" shouted the Oak over the storm.

But the storm only got stronger. The wind gave one GIANT PUSH. The proud Oak Tree held his breath. He tried to stand firm. He would not bend!

He looked down at the fallen Oak with kindness. "You see," the Bamboo whispered gently. "Sometimes, it is better to bend than to break. Being flexible is its own kind of strength."
