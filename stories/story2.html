The Towering Oak and the Willowy Bamboo

(Level: Medium)

In a wide, grassy valley, there stood a magnificent Oak. He was enormous. His trunk was thick and rugged, and his branches spread out like a crown, reaching for the sky.

He was very proud of his power. "Look at my foundation!" he would boom to the other plants. "My roots go deep, and my wood is hard. I am the strongest thing in this valley. I fear nothing!"

Growing not far away was a grove of slender Bamboo. They were tall and green, but very thin. When the smallest puff of wind came by, they would shiver and sway, their leaves making a soft shhh-shhh-shhh sound.

The towering Oak looked at the Bamboo with scorn. "You poor, flimsy creatures," he scoffed. "You are always dancing and bowing. You bend to every little breeze! Why don't you grow a strong trunk like mine? You must be terrified of everything."

The Bamboo rustled kindly in reply. "Oh, Mr. Oak," one of them whispered. "We are not afraid. We just know that it is wise to be flexible. We do not mind bowing."

"Hmph!" grunted the Oak. "Bowing is for the weak. I never bow. I stand firm!"

One evening, the sky turned a dark, angry purple. A great typhoon was coming. The wind didn't just blow; it HOWLED. It screamed through the valley, pulling at everything. The rain lashed down sideways.

"I will not be moved!" shouted the Oak. He puffed out his branches and held his massive trunk rigid against the blast. He was a stone wall against the wind. "Let the storm try! I am stronger!" The wind hit him, and he groaned, but he did not bend.

Meanwhile, the Bamboo were doing something very different. As the furious wind attacked, they did not try to fight it. They bent over, lower and lower, until their graceful tops nearly touched the muddy ground. They let the howling gale rush right over their backs. They looked very small and weak, but they were safe.

The storm grew even more powerful. It saw the proud Oak standing tall and hit him with all its force. "NO!" bellowed the Oak, trying to hold his ground.

But the wind was stronger. It pushed and pushed against the rigid trunk. There was a terrible, splintering CRACK! Because he would not bend, the mighty Oak snapped in two. With a final, ground-shaking THUD, the strongest tree in the valley toppled over, his great crown crashing into the earth.

The next morning, the storm was gone. The sun came out and shone on a peaceful, clean-washed valley. But the magnificent Oak was lying on the ground, his hard wood shattered and his roots torn up.

And just a few steps away, the slender Bamboo slowly stood back up. They were a little wet and muddy, but they were completely unharmed. They rose up, thin and green, and began to rustle softly again in the gentle morning breeze.
